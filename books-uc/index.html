PREAMBLE

While translating Umdatul Ahkam (288), I encountered the Hadith of Wasiyya from Bukhari (2738): "It is not befitting for a Muslim who has anything concerning which a will should be made, to spend two nights without having a written will with him." 'Abdullah bin 'Umar said: "Ever since I heard Allah's Messenger ï·º say that, I have not spent a night without having my will with me."

It is prohibited to make Wasiyyah to an heir, either giving them or depriving them of their rightful share through Faraid (Islamic inheritance law), just as it is forbidden to make Wasiyyah to allocate more than 1/3rd of one's wealth as Sadaqah. The Messenger ï·º said: "Allah has given each person their (inheritance) rights and no will can be made to an heir." (Tirmidhi 2120). Any stipulation contrary to what Allah has revealed is invalid.

I do not wish that an Ash'ari who does not know where his Rabb is, or a westernized secularist with no Deen, to lead the prayer over me. Whoever prays an obligatory prayer behind them should repeat their prayer afterward. Rather, it should be Ahlul Hadith who lead the prayer over me, as they are Ahlu Sunnah wal Jama'ah and the true followers of the Salaf. The Sunnah dictates that the deceased should be buried promptly, without unnecessary delays for the sake of a larger congregation. There is no prayer in absentia in the Sunnah unless it was not offered for the deceased where they died.

I referred to the wording of how Wasiyya should be written from Sunan ad Darimi (3092) and Baihaqi (12807).

LAST WILL AND TESTAMENT OF ABU YAHYA, MOHAMED ASHRAAF IBRAHIM

I, Abu Yahya, Mohamed Ashraaf Ibrahim, being of sound mind and memory, do hereby make, publish, and declare this to be my Wasiyyah.

I testify that there is no Ilah worthy of worship except Allah, Who has no partner, and that Muhammad is His slave and Messenger, and that the Final Hour will come without any doubt, and that Allah will resurrect those in the graves.

I make Wasiyya for my children and my family, to have Taqwa of Allah, reconcile among themselves, and obey Allah and His Messenger, if they are true believers.

I make Wasiyya to them as Ibrahim and Yauqoob made Wasiyya to their children: "O my sons, indeed Allah has chosen for you this religion, so do not die except while you are Muslims."

I make Wasiyya to them to remain close to those who follow the Salaf and to be their brothers and sisters in Deen. Indeed, chastity and truthfulness are more pious and honorable than indecency and lies.
