Frequently Asked Questions

For questions that are not listed here please reach out and contact us.



1. Who made and maintains hadithmv?

(Abu Yahya) Mohamed Ashraaf Ibrahim. Student of the Hadith Faculty, Islamic University of Madina.


2. When was hadithmv started?

Jul 17, 2017.


3. Is there an app?

Yes. hadithmv is available online on the web. It is also available offline as an Android and Windows app.


4. Is there an iOS app?

Not yet. However, iOS users can get the same functionality as the android app from the web version of hadithmv.


5. How do I continue from the last place I was at a later time?

You can use the web version of hadithmv, the page will stay as is even if you close the browser. Alternatively you can save the URL.


6. Why not make seperate apps for all of the books?

The purpose of hadithmv is to have everything accessible in one place. A centralized, unified approach is the most efficient in terms of size, speed and development. In short, to avoid redundancy and bloat. Otherwise a hundred books would be a hundred apps on each platform.


7. Why is it called hadithmv? and why are there things other than hadith on it?

It started out as a website providing the Dhivehi translation of Nawawi's 40 hadith, then later developed into much more than that. While the main focus will always be on reviving the Sunnah and making it accessible for everyone, it became important to include everything that helps in achieving that goal. The most important of which would be the Quran without a doubt, followed by books on the correct Aqida. Supporting language material for learning Arabic and translating was also found to be indispensable.
