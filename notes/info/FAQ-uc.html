How do I continue from the last place I was at a later time?

In the web version of hadithmv, the page will stay as is even if you close the browser, or alternatively you can save the URL. Otherwise you can note down the page number where you were and later input that number to continue exactly from there, and this method can also be used in the app.


Is there an app?

Yes. While hadithmv is primarily available online on the web, it is also available offline as an Android and Windows app.


Is there an iOS app?

Not yet. However, iOS, macOS and Linux users can get the same functionality as the Android app from the mobile web version of hadithmv. In sha Allah there will be an iOS app, contact us if you would like to help with making one.


Can I join the hadithmv project?

The hadithmv team is ever expanding. Currently, we are looking for people who have skills in three key areas: Dhivehi, Arabic, and programming. Roles include translating, proof-reading and coding. Feel free to contact us for more information if you would like to know more about joining hadithmv.


What characteristics are you looking for in volunteers?

Primarily, applicants should be: optimistic, self-motivated, responsive, able to meet deadlines, and not feel offended when corrected.


How else can I help hadithmv?

One way is by rating the Android app. Note that you can rate without showing your name if you don't write anything. Another way is by sharing hadithmv with your friends. If you ever wanted to spread Islamic knowledge in Dhivehi, or maybe even thought about distributing a great many books to Maldivians all around the world, then sharing hadithmv is as easy as it gets. Furthermore, the Prophet Ô∑∫ said: ‚ÄúWhoever guides someone to goodness will have a reward like one who did it.‚Äù -Sahih Muslim 1893


Who else has participated in the hadithmv project?

hadithmv has had various helpers over time. Most of them are friends and colleagues from the Islamic University of Madinah, as well as other students of knowledge and people who are skilled in the Dhivehi and Arabic language, and others who help with the code itself. For a list of contributors please click here. Note that here at hadithmv, we don‚Äôt use classism, and instead follow the all contributors specification. We realize that people like you and me are giving themselves and their free time to contribute in so many ways, and as such, we dislike making people feel their worth is ranked by titles and professions, or that some people are below others, like other bygone projects have done.


Why is it called hadithmv? and why are there other than hadith in it?

It started out as a website providing the Dhivehi translation of Nawawi's 40 hadith, then later developed into much more than that. While the main focus will always be on reviving the Sunnah and making it accessible for everyone, it became important to include everything that helps in achieving that goal. The most important of which would be the Quran, followed by books on the correct Aqida. Supporting language material for learning Arabic and translating was also found to be indispensable.


What can I do on hadithmv?

Primarily you can read various Islamic translations of Quran, Hadith, Aqidah, Duas etc. You can also use hadithmv like a dictionary. You can find the source references for these books. You can lookup texts in Dhivehi as well as Arabic, and can search even without Arabic diacritics. You can copy and paste the translations to and from any device, through any kind of media. You can select how many items to display on the screen at one time. You can jump to any page. You can use filter options to see only specific texts. You can show and hide different types of text. You can use keyboard shortcuts on the desktop, etc.


I messed up the search / settings on hadithmv, how do I reset things?

If you are on the app, then simply closing the app fully and then opening it again will reset it. If you are on the web version then clear the search box, clear the selected options, set the number of items viewed to default, and deselect the extra fields you have chosen to show. Or you can just fully clear the cache from the browser and revisit to that page anew (Ctrl + Shift + R).


Is hadithmv free?

We are providing our content free of charge, and it will remain that way, in sha Allah. Knowledge does not decrease when shared and made accessible to all, it only increases.


Who funds hadithmv?

hadithmv is currently funded by the developer and no one else.


How is hadithmv different?

It is the first program of its kind in the Dhivehi language, and as far as we are aware, the only. Another game-changer is that here at hadithmv, we fit with the times. Our content loads as fast as can be, and uses a fraction of your data when it does. In more ways than one, hadithmv saves you time and money, because every byte counts. We like to keep things minimal yet modern. We don't spend our time creating flashy animations or searching for filler photographs to go with what to write. We realized early on that a one size fits all approach and readily available templates does the job much more efficiently. This way we can focus on what is important, delivering quality content instead of worrying about trivial secondary matters. Nor do we limit ourselves to bulky hard-coded PDFs that are hard to read on mobiles, and even harder to search text in. Rather our content is responsive and easily searchable, with custom options like copying or choosing how many items to display. Furthermore, we don't do things in waves that come and go, or get people worked up on something for short bursts that inevitably fade away. Instead, we aim for steady work and sure outcomes that are designed to last. Also, while most books tend to be very expensive in the Maldives, we make sure all our physical books are extremely affordable, for the sake of spreading the knowledge. And when it comes to social media, we keep things bite-sized so subscribers can follow up on our content even on a busy schedule. These days, there is a lot less need to ferry people or books back and forth across the country, that ship has sailed long ago. And even PDFs are fast becoming dinosaurs. The generation of today expects whole libraries to fit responsively within their pockets and be able to use them easily and immediately within a snap, and hadithmv is well set to deliver on those expectations, in sha Allah.


Could you record video and audio to go with the hadith?

Fundamentally, we aim to create content for the source texts, as everything is built on that. Once that resource is readily available, it becomes easier for everyone else to create multimedia based on it. This is why we have chosen to invest most of our time and effort in this basic area, as it is more important in the long term than spending hours and hours recording and editing other media. As the years go by, images will become pixelized and blurry as they keep getting reuploaded and compressed over and over again. And videos will become outdated and search algorithms will push them further back as new ones come out to replace them. As for audio, one thing is how much of a hassle it is to go back and rerecord things whenever a change in script is called for, and for another, it is inevitable that Dhivehi text-to-speech engines will develop to a point where manually recording and editing clips will become all but irrelevant. Outlets, channels, groups, domains, movements, activities, events, speeches, presentations, captions, blog posts, one piece articles etc will all come and go. But without a doubt, one thing remains the same; the fundamental value of the core text, which will keep getting reused and built upon over and over again, and so that is precisely where we have chosen to commit to.


How does hadithmv make content so fast?

It is mainly due to modernized workflow and tools, we tend to automate things as much as possible. This allows us to create a weeks worth of content that otherwise would have taken others months. We avoid doing things over and over again and suffice with scripts wherever we can, as we find manual repetition to be extremely cumbersome and a waste of time. Inevitably, these methods will outstrip all others when it comes to performance and delivery, no matter the numbers, even though by the Grace of Allah we have writers such that some of them are worth 50 others. Furthermore we use custom software to keep up with our rapid releases, some of which we have mentioned or made available for others on the site.


Where is hadithmv based?

In the Maldives, although most of the code and content was written in Medina.


Who is hadithmv for?

Quite simply, everyone Maldivian. From students to teachers, children to parents, and seekers of knowledge to scholars, etc.


Is hadithmv affiliated with any organization, whether governmental or an NGO?

No, hadithmv is not affiliated with any organization. This project is run independantly and unaffected by the influences of third parties.


Are the translations correct?

In sha Allah. We strive to make them as accurate and understandable as possible. However, if you do find an inaccuracy somewhere, please report it to us so that we may correct it as soon as possible. Contact links can be found at the bottom of this page.


Are the translations approved by the Islamic Ministry?

The translated books approved by the Islamic Ministry will have an approval reference number on its page. While others maybe in the making or pending approval.


Are all the hadith here authentic?

If a hadith is from Bukhari or Muslim, then it is undoubtedly authentic. For any other book, the grading of a hadith scholar is required before saying it is authentic. Some references on hadithmv do include such gradings, while others do not have them yet.


How do you source your arabic text?

We usually rely upon the texts of the Shamela library program, unless specified otherwise. We look for the most accessible source for the text, as that is what most people will find easily available, then we use the best references we can find even if that is only in a hardcover book.


What numbering system do you use to reference hadith?

We usually use the indexing scheme of Muhammad Fuad Abdul Baaqi wherever possible, as it is the most commonly used and easiest to look up. Otherwise we use the numbering order found in the Shamela library program. Refer to the source book we used to know the numbering in place.


When I looked up the reference number from a source, why did it not match?

Different editions and prints may use different numbering schemes. Make sure that you looked up the same source books that we ourselves took the numberings from.


What are hadith?

They are the sayings, actions, approvals as well as the physical characteristics and manners of Muhammad Ô∑∫, the final Prophet sent by Allah. Hadith is also called Sunnah. After the Quran, hadith is the second source of all sharia legislations as well as behavioural etiquette standards in Islam. We can only know what we should and should not do through the Messenger Ô∑∫. Following the Messenger Ô∑∫ is the only way to salvation and all that is good, and choosing any other path is falling into wretchedness and destruction, in this world as well as the next.


What is Takhrij?

Takhreej is referencing the hadith text to the source book from which it is originally found.


Is there a dark mode / night theme?

The site automatically switches the theme based on your system preferences, however the app does not as of yet.

Why is the site different from the app?

The site gets updated first, so the latest changes will always be available there. We only roll updates to the app later, so that we can be sure everything worked out smoothly on the site beforehand. This is why the site will always have a higher version than the app.


Why is a translation different from before?

We might have corrected it or later wrote a better translation. Here at hadithmv we do not believe in writing content once and then forgetting it. This is not just another book that doesn't get updated, or an article that can never change. Rather it is possible to edit and update the text of hadithmv anytime. Think of it in terms of a 'living standard'. We are always striving to improve and build upon the translations, which are too important to just write once and then abandon later on.


Why is a book on hadithmv incomplete?

Most likely because it is still in the process of being written and checked, it will be fully available once ready, in sha Allah.


Could I know more about a particular book?

Check if there's a link to an about page where that book is found. Otherwise feel free to contact us for more information.


Which books are currently available?

The content we have drafted and kept in-waiting is actually more than the content we show on the site. Once internal checks have progressed to a point that we are happy with, it becomes available for public release.


Which books will be available soon?

We focus mainly on hadith. But we also include content on Quran, Aqida, Duas and language when we feel it appropriate. So exactly what book we are working on will constantly be changing, consider joining the translation team to be in on the loop.

What is your Aqeedah?

My Aqeeda is that of Ahlu Sunnah wal Jama'ah. I follow the Quran and Sunnah upon the understanding of the Companions. I affirm the Names and Attributes of Allah as He and His Messenger Ô∑∫ did, without changing their wording, interpreting them as metaphorical, denying them, likening them to the creation, or asking how they are. I hear and obey even if a slave is put over me, and I give advice to those in authority privately and not on the streets. This is what I was taught at the Islamic University of Madina. And as such, we will not allow any other creeds into the translations of hadithmv. Another important reason why this project had to happen was because of the widespread Ash‚Äôari interpretations that had plagued the existing translations of Islamic books such as the Quran, Riyadussaaliheen, Sahih Muslim, etc. This had gone on for long enough with no one being able to cleanse them of the various inserted deviations and restore them to their original purity. Despite Aqida being a major issue, problems such as these had still persisted throughout the generations of Maldivians as well as over 300 Islamic graduates residing in this small country of ours. May Allah make us of those who solve this problem once and for all, Ameen.


Why do you call yourself Yahyas Dad / Abu Yahya?

Why not make separate apps for all of the books?

The purpose of hadithmv is to have everything accessible in one place. A centralized, unified approach is the most efficient in terms of size, speed and development. In short, to avoid redundancy and bloat. Otherwise a hundred books would be a hundred apps on each separate platform, which would be very inefficient to maintain. Imagine if the Shamela Islamic Library had a seperate program / app for each of their books with multiple database engines to be looked after. Now you can understand exactly how difficult that would be to use for the end user, and how ineffective that would be to develop as any programmer would know.


I learned about a hadith, can I issue fatwa or answer fiqh questions?

No, that is only something the scholars can do, because the Sharia of Allah is not spoken of except with knowledge. While hadithmv is intended to be a resource for various Islamic texts, one or two hadith alone is not sufficient for layman to have formed a comprehensive opinion on a matter. Many individuals who haven not studied even the most basic of Islamic books or know even the slightest Arabic think that just because they have the internet, they are as capable as any scholar. This is nothing but a complete and utter fallacy. You cannot come up with your own reasoning nor make up your own analogy when it comes to Islamic rulings. That is simply not how things work in this Deen. Anything related to fiqh or fatwa should always be directed to the real scholars. Otherwise, one will only go astray, while leading others astray as well.
