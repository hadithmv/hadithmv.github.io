Frequently Asked Questions

For questions that are not listed here please reach out and contact us.



What is the slogan of hadithmv?

The Maldivian Hadith translation platform.


What is hadith?

They are the sayings, actions, approvals as well as the physical characteristics and manners of Muhammad ï·º, the last Prophet sent by Allah. Hadith is also called Sunnah. It is the second source of all sharia legislations as well as behavioural etiquette standards in Islam. We know what we should and should not do only through the Messenger ï·º. Following the Messenger ï·º is the only way to salvation and all that is good, and choosing any other path is falling into wretchedness and destruction in this world and the next.


Is hadithmv on social media?

Yes, hadithmv can be found on Facebook, Viber, Telegram, Instagram and Twitter. See the very bottom of this page for links.


What is Takhrij?

Takhreej is referencing the hadith text to the source book from which it is originally found.


Is there a dark mode / night theme?

The site automatically switches the theme based on your system preferences, however the app does not as of yet.


What is hadithmv, and what is it for?

Please read about that in detail here.


How is hadithmv different?

It is the first program of its kind in the Dhivehi language, and as far as we are aware, the only.


Where is hadithmv based?

In the Maldives, although most of the code and content was written in Madina.


How do I use hadithmv?

Please read about that here.


I have a suggestion / correction / question. How can I contact you?

Click on the "chat with us" button on the lower right of this page.


What fonts does hadithmv use?

Mvtypewriter and Yakout.


What is your Aqidah?

My Aqida is that of Ahlu Sunnah wal Jama'ah. I follow the Quran and Sunnah upon the understanding of the Companions. I affirm the Names and Attributes of Allah as Allah and his Messenger ï·º did, without changing their wording, interpreting them as metaphorical, denying them, likening them to the creation, or asking how they are. I hear and obey even if a slave is over me and I give advice to those in power privately and not on the streets. This is what was taught in the Islamic University of Madina.


How do you make content so fast?

It is mainly due to modernized workflow and tools, we tend to automate things as much as possible. This allows us to create a weeks worth of content that otherwise would have taken months had we done everything manually.


How is hadithmv licensed as a software?

Please read about that here.


What tools was hadithmv made with?

Please read about that here.


Does hadithmv have any mirrors, in case the main site is down?

Yes, click here, or here, or here, or here.


Why is some content missing from a book?

Most likely because still in the process of being written and checked, it will be fully available once ready.


What will happen to hadithmv if something happens to you?

Everything is arranged and we have protocols in place for even such scenarios. It has already been decided who takes over and continues the project, so no worries about that in sha Allah


Why is the site content different from the app?

The site is always updated first, the latest content will be available there. The app only gets updated a while after.


How can I help out with hadithmv?

We are looking for people who have skills in on or all of these three: Dhivehi, Arabic, and programming. Roles include translating, proof-reading and coding. Furthermore, applicants should be self-motivated, responsive, able to meet deadlines, and not feel offended when corrected. Contact us for more information if you want to join. You can also help by rating the app and sharing hadithmv with your friends.


Is hadithmv free?

Of course. It has and always will be free, in sha Allah.


Which books are currently available?

You can find those on the main page. We might have some other books ready, but awaiting further checks before being shared publicly.


Which books will be available soon?

We focus mainly on hadith. But we also include content on Quran, Aqida, Duas and language when we feel it appropriate. So what we are currently working on will constantly be changing, consider joining the translation team to be in the loop.


How do you source your arabic text?

We usually rely upon the texts of the Shamela library program, unless specified otherwise. We look for the most accessible source for the text, as that is what most people will find easily available, then we use the best references we can find even if that is only in a hardcover book.


I would like to know more about a particular book?

Check if there's a link to an about page where that book is found. Otherwise contact us for more information.


Are the translations correct?

In sha Allah. We do our best to make them as accurate and understandable as possible. But this is still a human effort, and newer content is more likely to have typos or errors than older, more established ones. Please report any inaccuracies so that we may correct them as soon as possible.


Are the translations approved by the Islamic Ministry?

The books approved by the Islamic Ministry will have a reference number on its about page. Others maybe in the making or pending approval.


Who made and maintains hadithmv?

(Abu Yahya) Mohamed Ashraaf Ibrahim. Student of the Hadith Faculty, Islamic University of Madina.


Why do you call yourself Yahyas Dad / Abu Yahya?

Because that is my Kunyah, which is a kind of Arabic nickname where a person is called after their oldest child.


Who else participates in the hadithmv team?

hadithmv has had various helpers over time. Most of them are friends and colleagues from the Islamic University of Madina, as well as people who are skilled in language and programming. For a list of contributors please click here.


When was hadithmv started?

Jul 17, 2017.


Is there an app?

Yes. While hadithmv is primarily available online on the web, it is also available offline as an Android and Windows app.


Is there an iOS app?

Not yet. However, iOS users can get the same functionality as the android app from the web version of hadithmv. In sha Allah there will be an iOS app, contact us if you would like to help with that.


How do I continue from the last place I was at a later time?

You can use the web version of hadithmv, the page will stay as is even if you close the browser. Alternatively you can save the URL.


Why not make seperate apps for all of the books?

The purpose of hadithmv is to have everything accessible in one place. A centralized, unified approach is the most efficient in terms of size, speed and development. In short, to avoid redundancy and bloat. Otherwise a hundred books would be a hundred apps on each platform.


Why is it called hadithmv? and why are there things other than hadith on it?

It started out as a website providing the Dhivehi translation of Nawawi's 40 hadith, then later developed into much more than that. While the main focus will always be on reviving the Sunnah and making it accessible for everyone, it became important to include everything that helps in achieving that goal. The most important of which would be the Quran without a doubt, followed by books on the correct Aqida. Supporting language material for learning Arabic and translating was also found to be indispensable.
